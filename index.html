Laytime Research

What is Laytime?

Laytime refers to the period agreed upon in the charter party during which the loading and/or discharging operations must be completed. It is usually a defined time frame.

Laytime can be expressed in hours or days and is divided into "working days" or "running hours," depending on the agreement. There can also be terms in the CP that account for weather or other environmental adversaries that pause the counting of the laytime (for example if it rains heavily, cargo Operations stop therefore, laytime counting stops.)

There are variations on how laytime is counted which will be covered in a separate wiki.

Laycan

Laytime is not to be confused with Laycan. Laycan is a portmanteau of "Laydays" and "Cancelling Date". It specifies the time frame within which a ship must arrive at the loading port and be ready to load e.g. 1st to 5th November

Demurrage

Demurrage is a term used in the shipping industry to refer to a financial penalty imposed on the charterer when the laytime is exceeded. It compensates the shipowner for the delay the ship experiences in port beyond the allocated laytime.

Despatch

Despatch is the term used to refer to a payment made by the shipowner to the charterer when the loading or unloading of cargo is completed in less time than the agreed-upon laytime. It serves as an incentive for the charterer to expedite cargo operations.

What is NOR?

NOR stands for "Notice of Readiness." It is a formal declaration given by the master of the vessel to the charterer, indicating that the ship has arrived at the port and is ready to commence loading or unloading of cargo.

Key Features of NOR:

  • Commencement of Laytime: The issuance of NOR typically marks the starting point for laytime, as laid out in the charterparty.
  • Conditions for Tendering NOR: The ship must be at the agreed location and physically ready to load or discharge.
  • Formalities: NOR must be issued in writing and received by the charterer or their representative to be valid.
  • Acceptance of NOR: The charterer or the agent must acknowledge receipt of NOR for laytime to start officially.
  • Practical Readiness vs. Legal Readiness: Disputes can occur if these conditions are not clear.

Importance of NOR:

  • Clarity and Documentation: NOR provides a clear and documented point from which calculations regarding laytime and potential demurrage or despatch can be made.
  • Operational Coordination: It ensures all parties are aligned on when port operations should begin, allowing for better scheduling and resource allocation.

What is the Statement of Facts?

A "Statement of Facts" (SoF) is a detailed record of events and activities that occur during a ship's loading or unloading process at a port. It plays a crucial role in laytime calculations and the determination of any demurrage or despatch.

Key Features of a Statement of Facts:

  • Detailed Chronological Record: The SoF includes a comprehensive log of all significant events.
  • Content: Typical entries include arrival time, NOR tendering and acceptance, start and end times for operations, and details of any delays.
  • Verification: The SoF is usually verified and signed by the ship's master and the port agent or charterer's representative.

Impact on laytime Calculations:

  • Reference Document: The SoF serves as a crucial reference for calculating the laytime used.
  • Demurrage and Despatch: Based on the SoF, parties can determine if the laytime exceeded the agreed-upon duration or if operations were completed sooner.
  • Dispute Resolution: The SoF provides an objective record that can be reviewed to resolve issues fairly and transparently.
  • Accountability: By documenting delays and their causes, the SoF helps identify who bears the cost of delays.

What should a Laytime calculator do and why?

  • Accurate Laytime Calculation: Determine the total time spent on loading/discharging, considering stipulated laytime conditions in the Charter Party.
  • Integration of Events: Uses data from the Statement of Facts (SoF) to document events like Notice of Readiness (NOR) and interruptions.
  • Demurrage and Despatch Calculation: Computes any demurrage charges for exceeded laytime and assesses despatch payments for time saved.
  • Automation and Efficiency: Reduces manual errors and saves time by automating calculations for quick assessments.
  • Clear Financial Implications: Provides insights into the financial effects of delays and rewards for efficient operations.
  • Contractual Compliance: Helps ensure adherence to charterparty terms, reducing disputes over laytime calculations.

These points highlight how a laytime calculator aids in managing laytime and associated costs efficiently.
